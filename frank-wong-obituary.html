Early Life and Immigration

Wong’s journey began in Toi Shan, Kwang Tung, China, where he was born in December 1948. As part of his family’s fourth generation of immigrants, Wong’s ancestors paved the way for his eventual arrival in Canada. At the tender age of five, he left his homeland to live with his grandmother in Hong Kong. Determined to build a better future, Wong saved up enough money and immigrated to Canada at the age of nine. However, due to a case of mistaken identity, he arrived under the alias Woon Tong Chiu, unintentionally assuming the identity of other Chinese immigrants living under false pretenses.

Contributions to Red Deer

Upon settling in Red Deer in 1971, Wong dedicated over three decades of his life to serving the city as a city planner. His expertise and commitment to urban development played a vital role in shaping the landscape of Red Deer. In 2004, Wong’s passion for community service led him to run for a seat on the city council, where he served five terms until his retirement in 2021.

Wong’s impact extended far beyond his professional career.
