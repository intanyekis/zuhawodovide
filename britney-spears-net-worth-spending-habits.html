11 How It All Started For Britney Spears

Britney Spears entered the entertainment industry with her time on The Mickey Mouse Club

  • Britney was not cast on The Mickey Mouse Club when she first auditioned

Britney Spears began her rise to fame and fortune at a young age. She was born on December 2, 1981, in McComb, Mississippi.

As a child, she started taking dance and gymnastics classes, showing off how gifted she was. Her parents, Lynne and Jamie Spears, noticed right away how talented she was and helped her start her career. When she was 8, she tried out for the Disney Channel show The All New Mickey Mouse Club, but she was too young at the time.

Instead, Britney would star on an off-Broadway show titled Ruthless for two years and return for tryouts at The All New Mickey Mouse Club at age 11. This time, she made the cut. It was Britney's first experience working in the television business.

10 Becoming A Teen Pop Sensation

Britney Spears became a household name with "Baby One More Time"

  • Britney sold over 10 million copies of Baby One More Time

From the late 1990s through the early 2000s, Britney Spears' star took flight. Her breakthrough came in the late 1990s, when she signed with Jive Records and released her debut single, "Baby One More Time," in 1998. The single and its accompanying album were massive hits! Possibly even more popular was the music video that went along with it.

Britney was iconic; she was dressed like a schoolgirl and, in true Britney form, danced like nobody else can. She rose to international superstardom nearly overnight. The album of the same name sold over 10 million copies in its first year. Britney's image as a wholesome yet edgy teen pop icon captured the hearts of millions of fans.

Oops!... I Did It Again was released on May 16, 2000. "Oops!... I Did It Again" was also the featured title track, which became one of Britney's signature songs and an enormous hit. The album also included other notable tracks like "Lucky" and "Stronger."

Britney Spears' third studio album, simply titled Britney, was released on November 6, 2001. This album exhibited a more mature and adventurous side of Britney's music as she explored different musical styles. It included hit singles like "I'm a Slave 4 U" which she performed live at the 2001 VMA's with a huge albino python draped over her shoulders.

6 Britney Spears' Net Worth Grew With Her Las Vegas Residency

Britney Spears made $140 million from her residency

  • Britney's final performance grossed over $1 million, breaking records for Las Vegas residencies

"Britney: Live in Concert" took place at the Planet Hollywood Resort & Casino in Las Vegas, Nevada. Specifically, Britney Spears performed at The AXIS, a 4,600-seat theater within the resort. It was officially announced in September 2013, and it began on December 27, 2013. It continued for several years, with her final performance taking place on December 31, 2017. Britney's Las Vegas residency was a massive success that earned more than $140 million in revenue. Her final performance grossed $1.1 million, the highest ever reported for a single-theater Las Vegas residency show.

Britney had started a new residency in Vegas called "Domination" in 2019, but quickly canceled it due to her father's health concerns.

1 Britney Spears Should Have A Lot More Money

Britney Spears should have a fortune in the hundreds of millions

  • Britney's father, Jamie Spears spent a large chunk of Britney's money after she was placed under conservatorship
