Because of China’s one-child policy back in 2009 allowed the pair to have only one child. And the baby received on May 22, 2010, came in the form of a daughter. Both their parents were overjoyed after the birth of their baby girl, whom they named Yao Qinlei.

Chinese families were only allowed one child under the country’s “one-child policy.” 1980 marked the beginning of the Chinese government’s statewide rollout of the program, which lasted until 2016. The nation’s authorities felt that the current pace of population increase was unsustainable, so they decided to implement the program to slow it down.

Yao Ming Family Life, Parents And Childhood

On December 9th, 1980, Yao Ming was born in Shanghai in a family of three.

He is the only child of Yao Zhiyuan and Fang Fengdi, two of China’s most famous basketball players. Yao was much larger than the average Chinese newborn. At nine, he enrolled at a junior sports school and began playing basketball there. At the age of 13, he tried out for the junior team of the Shanghai Sharks.

In his first season playing for the senior squad, he had a scoring average of 10 points and an average of 8 rebounds. He had a foot injury during his second season, so his vertical jump was reduced to 10 centimeters.

Ming guided the Sharks to the championship game of the Chinese Basketball Association (CBA) in both his third and fourth seasons as the team’s head coach, but both times they were defeated by the Bayi Rockets. The team won their first CBA championship in Ming’s fifth season with the Sharks. Yao finished his career with Shanghai by making all 21 of his shots in the finals.
